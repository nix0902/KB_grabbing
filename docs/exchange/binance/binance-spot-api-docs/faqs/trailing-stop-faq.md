<h1>Spot Trailing Stop order FAQ</h1>
<h3 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_fMI7" id="what-is-a-trailing-stop-order">What is a trailing stop order?<a class="hash-link" aria-label="Direct link to What is a trailing stop order?" title="Direct link to What is a trailing stop order?" href="/docs/binance-spot-api-docs/faqs/trailing-stop-faq#what-is-a-trailing-stop-order">​</a></h3>
<p>Trailing stop is a type of contingent order with a dynamic trigger price influenced by price changes in the market. For the SPOT API, the change required to trigger order entry is specified in the <code>trailingDelta</code> parameter, and is defined in BIPS.</p>
<p>Intuitively, trailing stop orders allow unlimited price movement in a direction that is beneficial for the order, and limited movement in a detrimental direction.</p>
<p>Buy orders: <em>low</em> prices are good. Unlimited price <em>decreases</em> are allowed but the order will trigger after a price <em>increase</em> of the supplied delta, relative to the <em>lowest</em> trade price since submission.</p>
<p>Sell orders: <em>high</em> prices are good. Unlimited price <em>increases</em> are allowed but the order will trigger after a price <em>decrease</em> of the supplied delta, relative to the <em>highest</em> trade price since submission.</p>
<h3 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_fMI7" id="what-are-bips">What are BIPs?<a class="hash-link" aria-label="Direct link to What are BIPs?" title="Direct link to What are BIPs?" href="/docs/binance-spot-api-docs/faqs/trailing-stop-faq#what-are-bips">​</a></h3>
<p>Basis Points, also known as BIP or BIPS, are used to indicate a percentage change.</p>
<p>BIPS conversion reference:</p>






























<table><thead><tr><th>BIPS</th><th>Percentage</th><th>Multiplier</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>1</td><td>0.01%</td><td>0.0001</td></tr><tr><td>10</td><td>0.1%</td><td>0.001</td></tr><tr><td>100</td><td>1%</td><td>0.01</td></tr><tr><td>1000</td><td>10%</td><td>0.1</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>For example, a <code>STOP_LOSS</code> <code>SELL</code> order with a <code>trailingDelta</code> of 100 is a trailing stop order which will be triggered after a price decrease of 1% from the highest price after placing the order.</p>
<h3 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_fMI7" id="what-order-types-can-be-trailing-stop-orders">What order types can be trailing stop orders?<a class="hash-link" aria-label="Direct link to What order types can be trailing stop orders?" title="Direct link to What order types can be trailing stop orders?" href="/docs/binance-spot-api-docs/faqs/trailing-stop-faq#what-order-types-can-be-trailing-stop-orders">​</a></h3>
<p>Trailing stop orders are supported for contingent orders such as <code>STOP_LOSS</code>, <code>STOP_LOSS_LIMIT</code>, <code>TAKE_PROFIT</code>, and <code>TAKE_PROFIT_LIMIT</code>.</p>
<p>OCO orders also support trailing stop orders in the contingent leg. In this scenario if the trailing stop condition is triggered, the limit leg of the OCO order will be canceled.</p>
<h3 class="anchor anchorWithStickyNavbar_fMI7" id="how-do-i-place-a-trailing-stop-order">How do I place a trailing stop order?<a class="hash-link" aria-label="Direct link to How do I place a trailing stop order?" title="Direct link to How do I place a trailing stop order?" href="/docs/binance-spot-api-docs/faqs/trailing-stop-faq#how-do-i-place-a-trailing-stop-order">​</a></h3>
<p>Trailing stop orders are entered the same way as regular <code>STOP_LOSS</code>, <code>STOP_LOSS_LIMIT</code>, <code>TAKE_PROFIT</code>, or <code>TAKE_PROFIT_LIMIT</code> orders, but with an additional <code>trailingDelta</code> parameter. This parameter must be within the range of the <code>TRAILING_DELTA</code> filter for that symbol.</p>
<p>Unlike regular contingent orders, the <code>stopPrice</code> parameter is optional for trailing stop orders. If it is provided then the order will only start tracking price changes after the <code>stopPrice</code> condition is met. If the <code>stopPrice</code> parameter is omitted then the order starts tracking price changes from the next trade.</p>

<p>At <code>12:01:00</code> there is a trade at a price of 40,000 and a <code>STOP_LOSS_LIMIT</code> order is placed on the <code>BUY</code> side of the exchange. The order has of a <code>stopPrice</code> of 44,000, a <code>trailingDelta</code> of 500 (5%), and a limit <code>price</code> of 45,000.</p>
<p>Between <code>12:01:00</code> and <code>12:02:00</code> a series of linear trades lead to a decrease in last price, ending at 37,000. <p>Between <code>12:02:00</code> and <code>12:03:00</code> a series of linear trades lead to an increase in last price. In this case, the lowest price is 44,000 and if there is a 500 BIPS increase from 44,000 then the order will trigger. <p>Between <code>12:03:00</code> and <code>12:04:00</code> a series of linear trades lead to an increase in last price, ending at 46,000. This is an increase of ~454 BIPS from the order&#x27;s previously noted lowest price, but it&#x27;s not large enough to trigger the order.</p>
