The length of both upper and lower shadows may vary, causing the candlestick you are left with to either resemble a cross, an inverted cross, or a plus sign. Doji candles show the playout of buyer-seller indecision in a tug-of-war of sorts. As price moves either above or below the opening level during the session, the close is either at or near the opening level. **Описание:** This TradingView image displays a **candlestick chart** (a standard technical analysis tool) with a focus on a specific candlestick pattern: the **Doji**. Here’s a detailed breakdown:  


### 1. **Candlestick Chart (Main UI Element)**  
- **Candles**: Vertical bars representing price action over time. Each candle has:  
  - **Body**: The thick central rectangle.  
  - **Wicks (Shadows)**: Thin lines extending above (upper shadow) and below (lower shadow) the body, showing price extremes.  
- **Color Coding**:  
  - **Green Candles**: Close price > Open price (bullish, price rose).  
  - **Red Candles**: Close price < Open price (bearish, price fell).  
- **Doji Candle**: Highlighted (labeled “D”) with a small body (open ≈ close) and wicks of varying lengths, resembling a cross or plus sign.  


### 2. **Doji Tooltip (Information Box)**  
A dark gray tooltip explains the Doji pattern:  
- **Title**: “Doji” (pattern name).  
- **Description**: Explains that a Doji forms when a security’s open and close prices are nearly equal. It describes the candle’s appearance (cross/inverted cross/plus sign) and its meaning: *buyer-seller indecision* (a “tug-of-war” where price moves but closes near the open).  


### 3. **UI Purpose**  
- **Chart**: Visualizes price movements (open, high, low, close) over time to identify trends/patterns.  
- **Doji Tooltip**: Educates users on the Doji pattern’s formation and significance (e.g., potential reversal signals, indecision).
