# Understanding volume candle charts 
 On our [Supercharts](https://www.tradingview.com/chart/), you can access volume candles. They are similar to standard candles only in that they vary in thickness which depends on the trading volume. This chart type can help you more easily identify the time periods when the most trading has occurred and analyze candlestick patterns.

#### What are volume candles 

If you are familiar with standard candles, you may already know that each candle consists of a rectangular body that represents open and close prices and wicks/shadows/tails that indicate the highest and lowest prices during that period.

Volume is one of the most important metrics in trading. High volume may tell you whether the price movement is caused by market participants' interest in that asset and thus it's more likely to be the "true" price direction.

On the contrary, low volume may indicate that, for instance, [chart patterns](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000759289-how-to-read-chart-patterns/) are broken falsely, and price may return to its previous values.

#### Volume candles vs standard candles 

As mentioned earlier, the only difference between these two chart types is that volume candles are displayed with different thickness of their bodies.

As a quick summary of their benefits, volume candles:

- Give you a clear picture of open, high, low, and close prices
- Provide an easy to interpret relationship between price and volume
- Work with candlestick patterns

#### Enabling the chart type 

Once you are on [Supercharts](https://www.tradingview.com/chart/), open the сhart type menu on the upper toolbar, and select "Volume candles."

#### Volume candles settings 

To make the most of your volume candles chart, you can customize them according to your preferences and needs.

For that, find the gear "Settings" button on the upper toolbar and go to the "Symbol" tab.

The first section, "Volume candles," lists settings specific to this chart type:

- **Color based on previous close:** When enabled, the color of each candle will depend not of this candle's open and close prices but on the relationship of this candle's close price and the previous ones
- **Body:** Change candles' bodies' outlook for enhanced visual appearance
- **Borders:** Change the colors of the candles' edges
- **Wick:** Choose the preferred color of the candles' shadows

#### Candlestick patterns 

To maximize the utility of candlestick charts, you can use [candlestick pattern indicators](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000584462-candlestick-patterns/). To do that, go to the upper toolbar and select "Indicators" → "Technicals," choose "Patterns" and scroll down to find "Candlestick patterns indicators."

These automated tools detect specific candlesticks and patterns and provide insights into their potential implications. However, it’s best to always evaluate the broader context and not rely solely on automated indicators to make trading decisions.

#### Volume candles in a nutshell 

There are many different forms of charts, each enhancing your view on the markets.

Volume candles are a step forward toward the understanding of true price movements. Their visual appearance may help you avoid potential false signals of other technical analysis tools regarding trend changes or continuation.

In trading, there's nothing completely true while relying on a single source of information. TradingView recommends studying other chart types along with indicators and drawing tools to have a broader understanding of financial markets.

### **Overall Layout & Purpose**  
The diagram is split into two sections:  
- **Left**: *Bullish candle* (green, indicating price closed **higher** than it opened).  
- **Right**: *Bearish candle* (red, indicating price closed **lower** than it opened).  

The TradingView logo at the bottom confirms the source. The goal is to teach traders how to interpret candlestick patterns by identifying price movements (open, close, high, low) and visual features (body, wicks/tails).


### **UI Elements & Labels**  
Each candlestick is annotated with dashed lines and text to define its parts:  

#### **1. Bullish Candle (Left, Green)**  
- **Body**: The solid green rectangle. Represents the range between the **opening price** (bottom of the body) and **closing price** (top of the body). Since it’s bullish, the close > open.  
- **Wicks/Shadows**: The thin vertical lines extending above (top wick) and below (bottom wick) the body.  
  - *Top wick*: Shows the **highest price** reached during the period.  
  - *Bottom wick*: Shows the **lowest price** reached during the period.  
- **Opening Price**: Dashed line at the bottom of the body (price at the start of the period).  
- **Closing Price**: Dashed line at the top of the body (price at the end of the period).  
- **Arrow**: A light gray upward arrow to the left, symbolizing a **price increase** (bullish trend).  


#### **2. Bearish Candle (Right, Red)**  
- **Body**: The solid red rectangle. Represents the range between the **opening price** (top of the body) and **closing price** (bottom of the body). Since it’s bearish, the close < open.  
- **Wicks/Shadows**: Same as the bullish candle—thin vertical lines above/below the body.  
  - *Top wick*: Shows the **highest price** reached.  
  - *Bottom wick*: Shows the **lowest price** reached.  
- **Opening Price**: Dashed line at the top of the body (price at the start of the period).  
- **Closing Price**: Dashed line at the bottom of the body (price at the end of the period).  
- **Arrow**: A light gray downward arrow to the right, symbolizing a **price decrease** (bearish trend).  


### **Shared Labels (Both Candles)**  
- **Highest Price**: Dashed line at the top of the top wick (maximum price in the period).  
- **Lowest Price**: Dashed line at the bottom of the bottom wick (minimum price in the period).  
- **Body**: The central rectangle (core price range between open and close).  
- **Wicks/Shadows**: The thin lines (extremes of price movement beyond the open/close).  
- **Tails**: Alternative term for wicks (used interchangeably in trading).  


### **Purpose of Each Element**  
- **Body Color/Size**: Indicates trend direction (green = bullish, red = bearish) and strength (larger body = stronger price move).  
- **Wicks/Tails**: Show volatility (long wicks = high volatility; short/absent wicks = low volatility).  
- **Open/Close Prices**: Define the “core” price action (whether buyers (bullish) or sellers (bearish) dominated).  
- **High/Low Prices**: Reveal the full range of price movement during the period.

The clean, labeled design makes it easy to distinguish between bullish/bearish patterns and interpret price action at a glance.
