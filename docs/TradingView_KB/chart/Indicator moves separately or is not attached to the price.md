- [ Help Center ](/) - / - / [ Indicator moves separately or is not attached to the price ](/support/solutions/43000775579-indicator-moves-separately-or-is-not-attached-to-the-price/) # Indicator moves separately or is not attached to the price You might encounter a situation where an added indicator or script displays incorrectly: - The indicator moves independently from the candles when you scroll or zoom the chart. - The line "floats" across the screen and doesn't "stick" to the price bars. - The indicator appears as a flat line (compressed) at the very bottom or top of the screen, even though the calculations seem correct. - The indicator scale does not align with the price scale. This usually happens because the indicator is set to No Scale or is pinned to a different scale (e.g., the Left scale) than the main chart symbol. To synchronize the indicator with the price data, you need to pin them to the same scale. - Right-click directly on the indicator line or its name in the legend (top-left corner of the chart area). - Hover over the Pin to Scale menu option. - Check which option is currently selected: If No Scale is selected: the indicator will "float" freely and won't move with the chart. - If Left or Right is selected (but not the one the price is on): the indicator may look compressed or offset. - Select the same scale that your main chart is attached to. In most cases, this is Scale A (Pin to Scale A) or the Right scale. Once selected, the indicator and the price candles will share the same coordinate axis and move synchronously.
