- [ Help Center ](/) - / [ Knowledge base ](/knowledge-base/) - / Financials - / [ Cash Flow ](/support/folders/43000565037-cash-flow/) - / [ Cash From Operating Activities ](/support/solutions/43000553627-cash-from-operating-activities/) # Cash From Operating Activities What is Cash From Operating Activities? Cash From Operating Activities represents the amount of cash that a company gets from its ongoing, regular business activities, such as the production and sale of goods or the provision of services to its customers. Why is Cash From Operating Activities important? Cash From Operating Activities is important for determining the financial success of a company's core business. It shows how much cash is generated from a company's core business operations. How is Cash From Operating Activities calculated? Cash From Operating Activities is the sum of [Funds From Operations](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563886), [Extraordinary Item](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000564145) and [Changes in Working Capital](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000564147). What does Operating cash flow per share show? Per share metric shows the amount of operating cash generated per share. It helps investors evaluate how efficiently a company’s core business activities convert revenue into actual cash. It’s important when assessing sustainability and financial health.
