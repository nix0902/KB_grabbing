Total Debt represents all the interest-bearing obligations of the company, regardless of when these obligations are due for payments. These obligations are used to provide liquidity to bridge short-term obligations and finance long-term capital expenditures and operations. Total Debt can be shown as [Long Term Debt](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000553621) and [Short Term Debt](https://www.tradingview.com/chart/?solution=43000563554). Why is Total Debt important? Total Debt shows how much a company has to pay back to those who have lent them money. Total Debt is important because it shows how much financial pressure is on a company to pay back its creditors or how much debt they’ve been able to access to invest and grow. What does Total debt per share show? This metric shows the company’s total debt distributed across all outstanding shares. It helps investors assess leverage on a per-share basis.
