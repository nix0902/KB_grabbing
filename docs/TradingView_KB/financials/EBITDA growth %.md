- [ Help Center ](/) - / [ Knowledge base ](/knowledge-base/) - / Financials - / [ Statistics ](/support/folders/43000572993-statistics/) - / [ EBITDA growth % ](/support/solutions/43000734968-ebitda-growth/) # EBITDA growth % Percentage EBITDA growth is the percentage change in [EBITDA](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000553610) over specific time periods. It is a measure of how much EBITDA has increased or decreased compared to the previous year or quarter. Different time periods for percentage EBITDA growth calculations include: - Annual YoY (Year-over-Year): Compares EBITDA for the two most recent years, such as 2023 and 2022, to show the annual growth rate. - Quarterly YoY: Compares EBITDA for the most recent quarter (e.g., Q2 2024) with the same quarter from the previous year (Q2 2023) to show the quarterly growth rate. - TTM YoY (Trailing Twelve Months Year-over-Year): Compares the EBITDA for the last known TTM period (e.g., Q2 2024) with the TTM period one year ago (Q2 2023) to show the growth rate over the trailing twelve months. - Quarterly QoQ (Quarter-over-Quarter): Compares EBITDA for the two most recent quarters (e.g., Q2 2024 and Q1 2024) to show the growth rate from one quarter to the next. These calculations help investors and analysts assess the company's performance and growth trends over different time frames. Positive percentage EBITDA growth indicates an increase in earnings, while negative percentage growth suggests a decline in earnings compared to the previous period.
