Total Current Liabilities represent debt or other obligations that a company expects to fulfill within one year. How are Total Current Liabilities calculated? Total Current Liabilities are the sum of the following: - [Short Term Debt](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563554); - [Accounts Payable](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563619); - [Income Tax Payable](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563621); - [Dividends Payable](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563624); - [Accrued Payroll](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563628); - [Deferred Income, Current](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563631); and - [Other Current Liabilities](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563635).
