- [ Help Center ](/) - / [ Knowledge base ](/knowledge-base/) - / Financials - / [ Cash Flow ](/support/folders/43000565037-cash-flow/) - / [ Cash From Investing Activities ](/support/solutions/43000553628-cash-from-investing-activities/) # Cash From Investing Activities What is Cash From Investing Activities? Cash From Investing Activities represents the amount of cash that a company brings in from its investing activities. It includes any cash inflows or outflows from the company's long-term investments. Why is Cash From Investing Activities important? Cash From Investing Activities is important because it shows how good or bad a management team is at investing or deploying capital. A negative value of Cash From Investing Activities can show poor performance, but it also can be a sign of increased investment activities. How is Cash From Investing Activities calculated? Cash From Investing Activities is the sum of: - [Purchase / Sale of Business](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000564156); - [Purchase / Sale of Investments](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000564160); - [Capital Expenditures](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000564166); and - [Other Investing Cash Flow Items](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000564163).
