# Target price 
 The target price is an important metric used by analysts to project the future value of a particular stock. It is a forecasted value of the security in one year, calculated as the arithmetic mean of the estimates predicted by the consensus of FactSet analysts. This projection is based on a comprehensive analysis of various factors, including the company's financial health, market trends, industry performance, and broader economic conditions.

The target price serves as a benchmark for investors, offering insight into the stock's potential future performance. But it's important to note that while the target price provides valuable insights, it is not a guaranteed prediction. Market conditions can change rapidly, and actual stock performance may differ from the analyst's projection. Therefore, investors should consider the target price alongside other factors, such as their own financial goals, risk tolerance, and broader market analysis, before making investment decisions.
