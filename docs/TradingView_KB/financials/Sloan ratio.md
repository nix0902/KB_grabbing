Sloan ratio counts as [Net income](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000553617) minus [Cash flow from operations](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000553627) and [Cash flow from investing](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000553628), divided by [Total assets](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000553623). Formula: (Net income - Cash flow from operations - Cash flow from investing) / Total assets What does Sloan ratio mean? This ratio is based on an article by Richard Sloan, according to which companies with small or negative accruals outperform shares of companies with large ones. Additionally, if the Sloan ratio is between -10% and 10%, then the company is in a safe zone. Previous Previous Shares buyback ratio Next Next Enterprise value to EBIT ratio Launch Supercharts
