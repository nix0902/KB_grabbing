# Dividend treatment 
 Dividends in ETFs represent a portion of the profits distributed to shareholders. There are two main approaches to handling these dividends:

- *Distributes*: Dividends are paid out to shareholders in cash, typically monthly, quarterly, or annually. This option provides immediate income but may have tax implications.
- *Reinvests*: Instead of cash payouts, dividends are reinvested in purchasing additional ETF shares. This approach promotes long-term investment growth through compound interest but does not provide immediate income to shareholders.
