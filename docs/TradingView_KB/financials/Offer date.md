The offer date is the day when the IPO is officially priced. On this date, the company and underwriters confirm the final offering size. The offer date marks the formal completion of the deal, but it is not the first trading day. Why is it important? The offer date shows the moment when the IPO is officially completed and priced. It helps users understand when the offer price became final and when the company received the proceeds from the offering. It also provides context for the first trading day, since pricing usually occurs shortly before the stock begins trading.
