Shares outstanding is the total number of ETF shares currently available in the market. This includes shares held by institutional and retail investors and those used in market-making activities. Why is it important? - Liquidity and trading efficiency – a higher number of shares outstanding generally indicates better liquidity and narrower bid-ask spreads. - Fund size and investor demand – the number of outstanding shares reflects investor interest. - Net Asset Value (NAV) impact – shares outstanding affect the calculation of an ETF’s NAV per share.
