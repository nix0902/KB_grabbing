# Sector & Industry - Non-durables 
 According to the *FactSet Industries and Economic Sectors* classification, the *Non-durables* category includes sectors that produce consumer goods with a short lifespan, such as food, beverages, tobacco, household and personal care products, apparel, footwear, and other consumer sundries. These sectors focus on manufacturing products consumed or used up relatively quickly and requiring frequent replacement.

Consumer Non-Durables

Food: Meat/Fish/Dairy

Beverages: Non-Alcoholic

Beverages: Alcoholic

companies that are engaged in the production (manufacturing, bottling, and distribution) of malt beverages, distilled and blended liquors, beer, and other alcoholic beverages
 Tobacco

companies that are engaged in the stemming and drying of tobacco. In addition, it includes companies that manufacture and distribute cigarettes, cigars, and chewing tobacco products

Household/Personal Care

companies that manufacture residential cleansing products and consumer personal care products

Apparel/Footwear

companies that either knit, cut, and sew fabric into garments for men, women, teenagers, and infants or that process leather, rubber, or leather substitutes into footwear

Consumer Sundries

companies that manufacture consumer non-durables. These companies are not classified elsewhere
