Total Non-Current Assets are a company’s long-term investments, the full value of which will not be realized during the reporting year. Examples of non-current assets include investments in other companies, intellectual property (i.e. patents), as well as property, machinery and equipment. How are Total Non-Current Assets calculated? Total Non-Current Assets are the sum of the following: - [Long Term Investments](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563639); - [Net Property, Plant & Equipment](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563657); - [Deferred Tax Assets](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563683); - [Net Intangible Assets](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563686); and - [Other Long Term Assets, Total](https://www.tradingview.com/?solution=43000563693).
