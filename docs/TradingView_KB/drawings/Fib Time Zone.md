Fibonacci time zones only indicate potential areas of importance related to time. No regard is given to price. The base interval is set by drawing a trend line between price highs or lows. The Time Zone tool then plots a series of vertical lines at increasing intervals. Fibonacci Time Zone parameters are defined by the Fibonacci Sequence. Starting with 0 and 1, each number is the sum of the two prior numbers (1+2=3, 2+3=5, 5+8=13 8+13=21 etc…). The main function of the Fibonacci Time Zone tool is to forecast or anticipate potential reversals based on elapsed time. The first few Fibonacci numbers create relatively tight Fibonacci Time Zones at the beginning of the chart. Because of this it may be necessary to ignore the first few zones. After the first few zones, the zones expand quickly and allows some breathing room as the sequence unfolds. The basic theory behind Fib Time Zones is that potential reversal points can be found by looking ahead to the starting point of new zones.
