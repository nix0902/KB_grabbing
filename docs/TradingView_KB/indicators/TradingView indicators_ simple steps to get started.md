Analyzing changes in such values allows predicting the future movement of the market. The values of the indicators are displayed on the chart as lines, columns, points, geometric figures, and other visual elements. **CONTENTS:** - [How to access TradingView indicators](#How-to) - [Types of indicators](#Types) - [Indicators available on TradingView](#Available) [Favorites](#Favorites) - [Personal](#Personal) - [Technicals](#Tech) - [Financials](#Fin) - [Community](#Com) How to access TradingView indicators When you are on [Supercharts](https://www.tradingview.com/chart/), click on the Indicators button on the top panel of the chart. From here, select an indicator and it will appear on the chart. Its name, parameters, and values will be displayed in the chart legend. Types of indicators Indicators help you assess the probability of the future price movements and build your trading strategy based on the historical data. Different indicators aim at different goals. Technical analysis allows for diverse trading styles, for which it's essential to evaluate trend, price, and volume conditions. - Trend direction - Momentum - Increase or decrease in volume - High or low volatility - Support and resistance levels [Relative Strength Index (RSI)](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000502338-relative-strength-index-rsi/) is one of the most popular technical indicators, appreciated and used by many traders. As a momentum indicator, it helps to gauge the strength of the recent price movement and spot the potential trend reversal. It's plotted on the scale from 0 to 100. Values can tell us about different states of the market. - &lt; 30 — oversold - &gt; 70 — overbought From the chart, you can see that it almost follows the direction of price, but the key here is to apply this knowledge to predict what's coming next. Financial indicators help you analyze a company's economic and financial health. They are based on the data derived from a company's corporate reports. - Income statement - Balance sheet - Cash flow When you hover your cursor over the financial data, you can find the question mark to the right of it pressing which will open the corresponding article in the Knowledge base. Head there to find more detailed information about [financial statements](https://www.tradingview.com/support/categories/financials/). Indicators available on TradingView TradingView comes with hundreds of built-in indicators and strategies and 100,000+ [indicators created by our community](https://www.tradingview.com/scripts/). You'll find them all in the Indicators menu, let's see what else is in there. Favorites This tab displays the indicators, metrics, and strategies you've chosen as your favorites. If you haven't, this tab will be empty. To mark an indicator as favorite, click on the star icon to the left of it in the list. Personal This tab displays the indicators written by you using Pine Script® — a programming language developed by TradingView. Visit the[ Pine Script page](https://www.tradingview.com/pine-script-docs/welcome/) to learn more about making the most of your trading with the only programming language made by traders for traders. To learn more about [available strategies](https://www.tradingview.com/support/folders/43000587406/), feel free to visit our Knowledge base - **Profiles:** These indicators help analyze price distribution and market dynamics over a specified chart interval - **Patterns:** Repeatable formations occurring on charts are known as patterns. Financials [Financial data](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000543506-what-is-financial-data/) is based on a company's performance and used in fundamental analysis to predict future prices. - **Income statement:** Provides information on a company's income, costs, profits, and losses over a period of time - **Balance sheet:** Helps to explore a company's assets, liabilities, and shareholder equity. Used to evaluate a company's capital structure - **Cash flow:** Allows to analyze the cash coming in and going out of a company at a specific point in time - **Statistics:** Lets you compare, contrast, and study the valuation of a specific company using financial ratios like price-to-earnings, price-to-sales, and price-to-book Community The Editor's picks, Top, and Trending tabs contain indicators created with the Pine Script by other TradingView users. Try them out to optimize your trading strategy. Indicators in a nutshell On TradingView you can find multiple indicators for any goal you're set to achieve. Whether you want to analyze a company's performance for a long-term investment or you're an active trader looking for sophisticated instruments. **Описание:** This TradingView chart displays the **BTC/USD (Bitcoin/U.S. Dollar)** pair on a **1-day (1D)** timeframe, with a candlestick price chart and an **RSI (Relative Strength Index)** indicator below. Here’s a detailed breakdown of UI elements:  


### **Top Bar (Header)**  
- **Symbol & Timeframe**: `BTC/USD` (left) with a `+` (add symbol), `D` (1-day timeframe), and `⚖️` (exchange/symbol info).  
- **Tools**: `Indicators` (add technical indicators), `W` (workspace), `Alert` (price alerts), `Replay` (backtest mode), and navigation arrows (`←→`).  
- **TradingView Menu**: `TradingView` (account/settings), `M/M/S` (market/minute/second data), and icons for `Refresh`, `Settings`, `Fullscreen`, `Screenshot`, and `Publish` (share chart).  


### **Price Chart (Main Area)**  
- **Candlestick Chart**: Green candles = price increase; red = decrease. Shows Bitcoin’s price action over months (Nov 2024–Apr 2025).  
- **Price Data**:  
  - `O 112,756.35` (Open), `H 113,366.37` (High), `L 111,599.30` (Low), `C 113,009.87` (Close).  
  - `+251.02 (+0.22%)`: Daily price change (green = gain).  
- **Y-Axis (Right)**: Price scale (55,000–115,000 USD).  


### **RSI Indicator (Below Price Chart)**  
- **Label**: `RSI 14 close` (14-period RSI, using closing prices).  
- **Values**: `45.33` (current RSI) and `55.75` (prior value).  
- **Lines**: Purple (RSI line) and yellow (RSI moving average).  
- **Overbought/Oversold Zones**: Light purple shaded area (30–70 range, with 30/70 dashed lines).  


### **Left Sidebar (Tools)**  
Icons for:  
- `+` (add object), `Trendline` (draw lines), `Bars` (volume), `Correlation` (asset correlation), `Fibonacci` (retracement), `Text` (add notes), `Erase` (remove objects), `Zoom` (magnify), `Magnet` (snap to price), `Lock` (lock chart), `Visibility` (show/hide), `Globe` (global market data), `Trash` (delete).  


### **Bottom Bar**  
- **Timeframe Buttons**: `1D, 5D, 1M, 3M, 6M, YTD, 1Y, 5Y, All` (switch timeframes).  
- **Calendar Icon**: Open date picker.  
- **Timestamp**: `17:13:17 UTC+4` (current time).  
- **Tabs**: `Pine Editor` (code indicators) and `Trading Panel` (trade execution).  


### **Right Sidebar (Features)**  
Icons for:  
- `Notes` (add annotations), `Clock` (time settings), `Depth` (order book), `Chat` (community), `Target` (price targets), `Drawing` (technical tools), `Calendar` (events), `Grid` (layout), `RSS` (feeds), `Help` (support).  


### **Key Purposes**  
- **Price Chart**: Analyze Bitcoin’s trend, support/resistance, and candlestick patterns.  
- **RSI**: Assess momentum/overbought/oversold conditions (RSI <30 = oversold; >70 = overbought).  
- **UI Elements**: Customize charts (indicators, timeframes), set alerts, trade, and share insights.  


This layout enables traders to analyze Bitcoin’s price action, momentum, and execute trades efficiently.
