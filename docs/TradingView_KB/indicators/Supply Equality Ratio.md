Supply Equality Ratio (SER) measures the distribution of wealth by comparing the supply held by the smallest addresses (less than 0.0000001% of supply) to the top 1% of addresses. Higher SER values indicate more democratic wealth distribution within the network. Why is "Supply Equality Ratio" important? This metric provides unique insights into inequality that traditional finance cannot measure, though it's important to note that one individual may control multiple addresses. Traders use SER to assess ecosystem health and decentralization progress, as higher equality typically correlates with more stable price action and reduced manipulation risk.
