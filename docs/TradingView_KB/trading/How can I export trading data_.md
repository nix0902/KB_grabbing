If you want to save your trading data you can use the function to export it in CSV format. To export data from your account, click on the "Export data..." button in the broker's dropdown menu: The information from each tab is exported individually. If you click on the export button, the information will be downloaded from the selected tab in the dropdown list. **Описание:** This TradingView interface displays the **Paper Trading** section, focused on account management and data export. Here’s a detailed breakdown:  


### 1. Top Navigation Tabs  
- **“Pine Editor”**: For creating/editing custom trading scripts (indicators, strategies).  
- **“Paper Trading”** (active, with a green dot): Simulated trading environment (no real money).  
- **“Trade”**: For live trading (real money).  


### 2. Header & Account Info  
- **“Paper Trading”** (with a dropdown arrow): Opens a menu (currently expanded) for account-related actions.  
- **“Account: Paper Trading USD”** (with a dropdown arrow): Shows the active paper trading account (currency: USD); clicking reveals account selection options.  
- **Gear icon**: Opens general TradingView settings.  


### 3. Expanded Dropdown Menu (from “Paper Trading”)  
A white dropdown with black/red text, containing:  
- **“Trading settings…”**: Configures paper trading parameters (e.g., order types, slippage).  
- **“Summary row”** (with a right arrow): Navigates to a summary view of the paper trading account.  
- **“Export data…”** (red border, highlighted): Exports paper trading history/data (e.g., trades, P&L) for analysis.  
- **“See the brokers list…”**: Lists brokers compatible with TradingView’s paper trading.  
- **“Log out Paper Trading”** (red text): Signs out of the paper trading account.  


### 4. Secondary Navigation Tabs (Right of Dropdown)  
- **“History”**: Accesses trade history (executed orders).  
- **“Account History”**: Tracks account activity (deposits, withdrawals, fees).  
- **“Trading Journal”**: Logs trading insights, strategies, or notes.  


### 5. Table Headers (Below Tabs)  
A table (partially visible) with columns for:  
- **“Qty”**: Quantity of assets traded.  
- **“Avg Fill Price”**: Average price at which orders were filled.  
- **“Unrealized P&L”**: Profit/loss on open positions (not yet closed).  


### Purpose of the Interface  
This screen manages a **paper trading account**—simulating trades without real money. The highlighted “Export data…” option lets users download trade history for review, while other menu items configure settings, view summaries, or log out. The table organizes trade-related metrics, and secondary tabs provide access to history, account activity, and a trading journal.
