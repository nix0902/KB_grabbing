# How to share your screen 
 With [TradingView screeners](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000766445-tradingview-screeners-walkthrough/), you can share your complete screener setup, including filters, sorting, columns, and watchlists. Your friends and other traders can view your custom configurations, make copies, and apply them to their own research.

#### Before you start sharing 

In short, there are just a few steps:

- [Create a TradingView account](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000591334-creating-your-account-and-selecting-your-avatar/) and log in
- Open a screener
- Create your own screen
- [Save it](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000718804-how-to-save-custom-screen/)

#### How to enable sharing 

To share your saved screen:

- Open it
- Click the screen menu (screen name drop-down)
- Toggle "Share screen"
- Click "Copy link"
- Share the link
 **! Note:** Each screen's sharing status is managed individually — enabling sharing for one screen doesn't affect others. 

#### Viewing a shared screen 

When someone opens your shared screen, they see a complete screener setup — with all filters, columns, and sorting exactly as configured by you. They can freely modify filters and sorting and make other interactions as if it were their own screen.
 **! Note:** Changes made to a shared screen are temporary until they are saved or the screen is copied. 
If you receive a link with a screen, you can:

- **Make a copy** in the screen menu
- **Save the screen** after making edits as a new one

After you save or copy the new screen, it belongs to you. Sharing is disabled by default, so make sure to enable it if you'd like to share.

#### Why you might not see the shared screen content 

In some situations, the shared screen may appear incomplete or empty. This can happen for several reasons:

- **You're not logged in:** Only logged-in users can view all data and make copies of shared screens. Anonymous users may see limited or no content until they sign in
- **Your level of subscription is not sufficient to see some filtered data:** If the shared screen uses filters, parts of the columns that require a higher level of subscription may be hidden
- **The screen includes an unshared watchlist:** If the original watchlist was not shared by the screen owner, the corresponding filter or table may appear empty
