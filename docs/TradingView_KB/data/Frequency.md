# Frequency 
 Frequency denotes the regularity with which data for a specific economic indicator is updated or reported. It represents the time intervals between successive data points. The frequency could be weekly, monthly, quarterly, or annually, depending on the indicator's nature and data availability. 

For example, employment figures might be reported monthly, while GDP data could be updated quarterly.
