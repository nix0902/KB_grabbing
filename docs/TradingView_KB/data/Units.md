# Units 
 Units of measurement specify the scale and format in which the values of an economic indicator are expressed. It articulates the quantitative representation of the variable. Units can vary widely and may include percentages, currency (such as dollars or euros), points, or other quantitative measures. 

For instance, inflation or unemployment rate are typically measured in percentage terms, while trade balance and current account may be expressed in the local currency.
