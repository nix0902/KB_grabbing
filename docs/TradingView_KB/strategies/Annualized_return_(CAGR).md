# Annualized return (CAGR) 
 Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) displays the rate of return of all trades, based on the initial capital, expressed as an annualized percentage. It shows how much your capital would grow per year if the performance during the measured period continued consistently over a full year.

### 1. Formula Description
The formula calculates the **Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR)** as a percentage, which measures the annualized growth of an investment over a specific period. The formula is:  

$$\text{CAGR (\%)} = \left( \frac{\text{Final Equity}}{\text{Initial Capital}} \right)^{\frac{365}{\text{Duration (days)}}} - 1$$  


### 2. Components of the Formula
- **CAGR (%):** The result (Compound Annual Growth Rate) expressed as a percentage. This represents the average annual return on an investment, accounting for compounding.  
- **Final Equity:** The total value of the investment at the end of the period (e.g., ending portfolio value).  
- **Initial Capital:** The starting value of the investment (e.g., beginning portfolio value).  
- **Duration (days):** The number of days the investment was held. The exponent $\frac{365}{\text{Duration (days)}}$ annualizes the growth by scaling it to a 365-day year (standard for annualized returns).  


### 3. Purpose of the Formula
CAGR is used to evaluate the performance of investments (e.g., stocks, portfolios) by smoothing out volatility and showing the average annual growth rate. It’s a key metric in trading, investing, and financial analysis to compare returns across different time periods or assets.
