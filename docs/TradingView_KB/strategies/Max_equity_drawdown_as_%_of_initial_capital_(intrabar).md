# Max equity drawdown as % of initial capital (intrabar) 
 This metric shows the maximum equity drawdown expressed as a percentage of your initial capital. It tells how much of the starting capital was lost during the worst equity decline within the period.

### 1. Formula Structure  
The formula is:  
$$\text{Max Equity Drawdown (\% of Initial Capital)} = \frac{\text{Max. Equity Drawdown}}{\text{Initial Capital}} \times 100$$  


### 2. Components & Purpose  
- **Left-Hand Side**: *“Max Equity Drawdown (% of Initial Capital)”*  
  This is the **output metric**—it represents the largest percentage loss of trading capital relative to the starting (initial) capital.  

- **Right-Hand Side**:  
  - *“Max. Equity Drawdown”*: The **absolute peak-to-trough loss** in trading equity (e.g., if your account drops from $10,000 to $8,000, the drawdown is $2,000).  
  - *“Initial Capital”*: The **starting amount of money** in the trading account (e.g., $10,000).  
  - *“× 100”*: Converts the ratio (drawdown / initial capital) into a **percentage** (e.g., $2,000 / $10,000 = 0.2 → 20%).  


### 3. No TradingView UI Elements  
This image contains **no buttons, charts, or interface elements** typical of TradingView (e.g., chart panels, order buttons, timeframes). It is purely a mathematical expression for a risk metric used in trading analysis.  


### Context (Why This Matters in Trading)  
Max equity drawdown measures the worst loss an account experiences, helping traders assess risk. A lower drawdown indicates more stable performance, while a higher drawdown signals greater volatility or risk.
