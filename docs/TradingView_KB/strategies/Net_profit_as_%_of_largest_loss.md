# Net profit as % of largest loss 
 This metric shows how much the strategy’s total net profit exceeds its largest single losing trade, expressed as a percentage. It helps to understand whether the overall performance of the strategy is strong enough to withstand individual large losses.

The image contains a single formula for calculating *“Net Profit as % of Largest Loss”*:  

$$\text{Net Profit as \% of Largest Loss} = \frac{\text{Net Profit}}{|\text{Largest Losing Trade}|} \times 100$$  


### 2. Explanation of the Formula  
- **Purpose**: This metric evaluates a trading strategy’s profitability relative to its worst loss. It helps assess risk-adjusted performance (e.g., how much profit is generated per unit of maximum loss).  
- **Components**:  
  - *Net Profit*: Total profit from all trades (after accounting for losses).  
  - *Largest Losing Trade*: The single trade with the biggest loss (absolute value, hence the `| |` notation for magnitude).  
  - The formula divides net profit by the magnitude of the largest loss, then multiplies by 100 to express the result as a percentage.
