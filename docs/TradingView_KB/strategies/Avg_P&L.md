# Avg P&L 
 The mean financial result generated by each closed position within the strategy. This metric tells, on average, how much money each trade contributes to (or subtracts from) the account balance.

**Exclusions**: This calculation is based strictly on realized results and does not account for the floating profits or losses of currently open positions.

**Strategic value**: This is a vital "viability" metric. For a strategy to be sustainable in live markets, the Avg P&L must remain significantly higher than combined commission paid and estimated slippage. If the Avg P&L is too "thin," the strategy risks being "eaten alive" by transaction costs.

Calculation Formula

The average is determined by dividing the Net P&L by the total volume of completed trades.

### **Content & Purpose**  
The formula shown is:  
**Avg P&L = Net P&L / Total Closed Trades**  

This equation calculates the **average profit or loss per closed trade** by dividing the total net profit/loss (from all closed trades) by the number of trades that have been completed (closed). It is a key metric for evaluating trading performance, as it quantifies the average outcome of each trade.
