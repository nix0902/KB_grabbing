# Return of max equity drawdown 
 Return of Max Equity Drawdown shows how much profit the account made relative to its largest drop in equity. It’s a measure of risk efficiency — essentially, how much return achieved for the maximum loss experienced. Higher values mean your strategy earns more per unit of risk.

### 1. Content of the Image  
The image shows a formula for calculating the **Return of Max Equity Drawdown**:  
$$\text{Return of Max Equity Drawdown} = \frac{\text{Total P\&L (Net Profit + Open P\&L)}}{\text{Max Drawdown (Intrabar)}}$$

### 3. Purpose of the Formula  
This formula is used in trading to measure the **risk-adjusted return** of a strategy:  
- **Numerator**: `Total P&L (Net Profit + Open P&L)` = Total profit/loss (combines closed profits and unrealized gains/losses from open positions).  
- **Denominator**: `Max Drawdown (Intrabar)` = The largest peak-to-trough decline in equity (including intrabar drawdowns, i.e., drops within a single candlestick/bar).  

The ratio quantifies how much return is generated per unit of maximum drawdown, helping traders assess if a strategy’s risk (drawdown) is justified by its returns.
