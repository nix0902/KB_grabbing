#  1.  Fund Your Exchange Wallet
  
To begin trading, you'll need to have funds in your exchange account. Here’s how to fund your wallet:
   -  
Open your exchange (e.g. Binance)
 
-  
Purchase crypto with a credit/debit card
 
-  
The funds will appear in your  Fiat and Spot  wallet

#  2. Making sure we have funds in the right wallet 
   Make Sure You Have the Right Funds in the Right Wallet   
Exchanges like Binance offer a variety of different market types and derivatives, and therefore it is essential that we introduce funds to the correct wallet type.

In Binance for example, there are two main markets in which you will end up trading with Cornix,  Spot and   Futures .

##  Spot  Trading-
  
To trade Spot markets, your funds must be in the  Fiat and Spot  wallet. This is where purchased crypto goes by default.

##  Future -  
  
Cornix supports two futures markets on Binance:
   -  
 USDⓈ-M Futures -  USDⓈ-Margined contracts are linear futures quoted and settled in  USDT  or  BUSD . These symbols are a type of cryptocurrency which represents the dollar amount. 
 
-  
 COIN-M Futures -  crypto futures contracts that are settled in cryptocurrencies (e.g. BTC, ETH). 

## How to Transfer Funds Between Wallets
   -  
Go to your  Fiat and Spot  wallet
 
-  
Click  Transfer 
 
-  
Select the destination wallet (USDⓈ-M or COIN-M)
 
-  
Choose the coin and amount
 
-  
Click  Confirm 

Here an example for how to transfer funds from your ' Fiat and Spot'  wallet to  USDⓈ-M Futures/COIN-M Futures  wallets.
