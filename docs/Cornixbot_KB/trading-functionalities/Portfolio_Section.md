# Portfolio Section
View and manage your assets and positions in one place
Written by Nikita Shirokov Updated over 3 months agoTable of contents
AssetsPositionsCreate a separate orderThe difference between Trades and Positions
In the portfolio section, you can see the current balance of your cryptocurrency assets.

Cornix gathers data from all your connected exchange accounts in one place, allowing you to view and manage your assets and positions in different ways.

At the top of this page, you can switch between tabs to see the distribution of your funds across Assets and Positions.

# Assets
In this section, you can see all the coins you currently hold across all connected exchanges.

From the Assets section, you can buy assets or sell the coins you own directly through Cornix.


Additionally, you can view:
-
A pie chart showing your asset allocation
-
An integrated TradingView chart

# Positions
In this section, you can view and close currently open positions across your accounts.
This is especially helpful when closing a trade using the Keep Coins option (learn more about this option here)
.

You can place Market / Limit / Stop Market / Stop Limit orders to close your position directly from Cornix:
-
Market – sell as soon as possible at the first available price in the orderbook
-
Limit – place a limit order at a specified price
-
Stop Market – specify a trigger price, and once reached, the position is closed with a Market order
-
Stop Limit – specify a trigger price, and once reached, a Limit order is placed

# Create a separate order
The Portfolio section also allows you to create a new order from scratch using the Buy button.


## The difference between Trades and Positions
Note that you will mostly operate with Trades on the Cornix platform. This gives you the flexibility to open and manage several trades of the same symbol at the same time, keeping the orders connected to each separate trade.

On the exchange side, you will usually see Positions instead of Trades.
This means several trades with the same symbol will appear as one position, with an adjusted average entry price.
