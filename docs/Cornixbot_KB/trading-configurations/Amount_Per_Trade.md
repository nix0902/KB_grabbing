# Amount Per Trade
How the “Amount per Trade” configurations work to determines how much of your portfolio is used for each trade
Written by Hadar Cornix Updated over 4 months agoTable of contents

The Amount per Trade setting determines how much will be invested in each trade.

Here are the 4 available options to set your amount per trade:

# Percentage
The percentage option will take a flat-out percentage out of your account's total portfolio. Meaning, both available and open trades amount, of the coin/contract that you are buying with.

For example, you have a portfolio of 1 ETH and 1000 USDT (500 available USDT and 500 USDT in use in open trades).
If you open a trade for a pair in which USDT is the coin that you will buy with and choose to use a 10% amount per trade, then the bot will open a trade in size of 100 USDT (10% out of your total USDT portfolio amounts - both available and in use in open trades).

⚠️ Note: If you use 100% Amount per Trade, especially in Futures trading, your trade might fail to open with the error message: “The required amount X is higher than your available amount.” This happens because there isn’t enough balance left for exchange fees. To fix this, try reducing the percentage slightly.

# Risk Percentage
Risk Percentage will enable you to use, in each trade, the amount you are willing to risk out of the traded coin portfolio value.
Theoretically, if your trade hits stop-loss, you will lose the amount that is equal to the percentage that you chose to risk.
This method can be used for effective risk management.

The calculation is based on the trade’s details and takes into account the potential loss of the trade (the distance from the entry price to the stop-loss price), then calculates the number of coins\contracts to be used in the trade.

## Risk Percentage position size formula:

​The values explained:
-
Risk %:
The chosen percentage to be configured on the Risk Percentage Amount per Trade.
-
Trade's Potential Loss %:
(Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)/Entry Price.
*When more than one entry applies, the entry Price will be calculated based on the weighted average entry price of the signal, after applying the bot's entry strategy.
-
Portfolio Size
Refers to the specific pair traded in the trade. For example, for a USDT pair, the calculation will take into consideration the amount of USDT in the portfolio.
-
In case the trade doesn't have a Stop Loss order, the position size will be the flat-out percentage out of your total portfolio size (both available and open trades amounts of the coin/contract that you are buying with.)
## Important Notes:
-
Trades that use Cross leverage and no Stop Loss cannot use Risk % configuration. You’ll see this message: “Using risk percentage amount type doesn't support cross trades without a stop loss. If you wish to change this behavior, consider adding a default stop to your configuration.”
This is because the stop-loss price is essential for calculating the amount in Risk%.
-
The calculation is based only on the stop order trigger, not on the actual market or limit order that executes after it. This means that the actual amount that you risk might result different than this calculated amount. The reason is that only the stop (trigger) is known when opening the trade.
-
If the desired position size based on the above calculation will result in an amount that is higher than your available amount, then the trade will open with a position size of the available amount.
-
The entry price used in risk calculation is based on the signal’s original entry with your Signals Bot entry configuration applied (e.g., number of entries, ratios).
-
Calculated Risk % amount might not be sufficient to open all entries. If after calculating the amount per each entry, some of them fall below the minimum order size on the exchange, those entries will be skipped automatically.

# Fixed BTC Amount
The amount to be used per trade will be equivalent to the BTC amount you choose (in the coin/contracts you are buying with).

For example, let's say you create a trade for LTC/USDT and you choose to use 1 BTC - the bot will open a trade of size X USDT that is equal to 1 BTC.

# Fixed USD Amount
The amount to be used per trade will be equivalent to the USD amount you choose (in the coin/contracts you are buying with).

For example, let's say you create a trade for LTC/BTC and you choose to use 100 USD - the bot will open a trade of size X BTC that is equal to 100 USD.

# Note: Amount per trade and Leverage:
On margin exchanges, the position size is generally calculated as your Amount per Trade × Leverage, unless the trade is specifically marked as a Leveraged Margin trade.
