intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h2 id="h_0a022e5625">What is it trying to solve?</h2></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>When trading automatically, you might experience cases where you might &quot;miss&quot; potential trades, when the first entry target remains unfilled even though the signal entry target was reached. <br/>A couple of cases where this might happen (assuming long signals) are:</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-ordered-nested-list"><ol><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>The signal was created when the symbol price was below the first entry price, but immediately as it was published many users entered the signal causing the price to increase again until it is above the first entry price. <br/>In that case, it&#x27;s possible that the price increased before some users were able to place the orders in the exchange and therefore, if the price does not fall back into the entry range, they might miss the trade.</p></div></li><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>The signal was created when the price was outside the entry price range and all the users that entered the signal end up creating a &quot;buy-wall&quot; which causes the price to hover around the first entry price but never cross it. <br/>In that case, many users have entry orders at the same price and some might end up getting filled while others might be left unfilled before the price starts to increase again and move away from the entry price.</p></div></li></ol></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Both cases are especially common where the signal is posted on a symbol with low-liquidity, where a relatively small trading volume can significantly affect price movement (and create walls in the order book).</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>The purpose of the &quot;First Entry as Market&quot; feature, is to help you avoid cases like the ones mentioned above, where the signal&#x27;s entry price is reached (or crossed) but your trade&#x27;s orders remain unfilled.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-subheading intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h2 id="h_10f116f900">How does it work?</h2></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>First, you&#x27;ll have to choose the &quot;<b>Maximum Price Cap (%)&quot;</b>, this will determine the percentage in which the bot will extend the first entry price.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>The default is 1%, and you can choose any percentage between 0.05%-20%.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Then, choose <b>when to activate</b> the feature<br/>​</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-image intercom-interblocks-align-left"><a href="https://downloads.intercomcdn.com/i/o/wrsw0nb0/1256315756/1bac55e83902a183df66387be9dd/image.png?expires=1772918100&amp;signature=2c9bc123a9081a038962bc030b2af74aa25b2562a415b8885f370956a5eb1432&amp;req=dSIiEMp%2FmIZaX%2FMW1HO4zSHZAmwB42cZCPFqiyrwBovDeuMKqwrmWQdGU17D%0AvIntON5iAS9dPRG%2Fb80%3D%0A" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer nofollow noopener"><img src="https://downloads.intercomcdn.com/i/o/wrsw0nb0/1256315756/1bac55e83902a183df66387be9dd/image.png?expires=1772918100&amp;signature=2c9bc123a9081a038962bc030b2af74aa25b2562a415b8885f370956a5eb1432&amp;req=dSIiEMp%2FmIZaX%2FMW1HO4zSHZAmwB42cZCPFqiyrwBovDeuMKqwrmWQdGU17D%0AvIntON5iAS9dPRG%2Fb80%3D%0A" width="757" height="186"/></a></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-unordered-nested-list"><ul><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Activate when <b>&quot;Entry Price Reached&quot;</b> will use the first entry target price as a trigger. It will wait for the entry price to be reached or crossed on the exchange, and only then will activate the percentage range for the entry price. <br/>In this case, Cornix <b>will only apply the</b> <b>percentage range </b>to the trade&#x27;s first entry once <b>at least one entry target is reached</b> in the signal. This means that the &quot;First Entry as Market&quot; will not help you in cases where the signal price never reached the entry price range.<br/>​</p></div></li><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Activate <b>&quot;Immediately&quot; </b>will create this range immediately when the trade is opened, without waiting for any trigger, or for the entry target of the signal to be reached. </p></div></li></ul></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>In both cases, when activated, Cornix will iteratively increase the first entry target price by small intervals until the order is filled, or until the maximum price as defined by the % value is reached (that maximum price will be equal to the original entry price + the set percentage). After reaching that maximum order price including the grace, Cornix will stop updating the order price and the order will remain open in the exchange until it is filled or canceled (just like any other regular order).</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>In effect, using this feature will allow you to extend the potential entry price range making it less likely to miss trades that reached at least one entry target.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-heading intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h1 id="h_008d80942d">Example for &quot;Entry Price Reached&quot;</h1></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Let&#x27;s take the following long signal of ADA/BTC:</p></div><div does not take effect and will not modify the trade in any way.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div In this case, let&#x27;s consider what would happen when the percentage is set and when it&#x27;s not set in your trading configuration:</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-ordered-nested-list"><ol><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>First Entry as Market<b> % is not set</b> - In this case, your trade will have a simple limit order placed at the first entry target price of 0.00002000.
