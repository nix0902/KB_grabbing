class="jsx-4bed0c08ce36899e article_body"><article class="jsx-4bed0c08ce36899e "><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>We know that as a channel admin, you highly care about how your channel&#x27;s performance is represented on the Marketplace. This article answers some of the most frequently asked questions about the <b>channel&#x27;s stats</b> displayed on your channel&#x27;s page.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p><b>Let&#x27;s dive in...</b></p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-subheading intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h2 id="h_f206c1b4c9">1. Why does my PNL change from one day to another?</h2></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Marketplace statistics are generated based on daily <b>backtesting runs</b> covering the past <b>1, 3, and 6 months</b>. Since the specific signals and trades included in the backtesting range can change each day (as older trades expire and new ones are added), fluctuations in the <b>PNL results</b> are expected, sometimes small, sometimes more noticeable.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>This behavior is normal and keeps the results only relevant for the tested timeframe. </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p><i>Read more about how the Marketplace statistics are calculated here:</i></p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p><a href="https://help.cornix.io/en/articles/9731166-marketplace-group-statistics">🔗 https://help.cornix.io/en/articles/9731166-marketplace-group-statistics</a></p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-subheading intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h2 id="h_8daa1ca810">2. Why are there fewer trades in my stats than the number of signals I post on my channel?</h2></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Even if you post many signals, <b>not all of them are executed</b> in the backtesting runs. This is because backtesting is simulated using a <b>$10,000 portfolio</b>, which represents limited available funds.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Once all funds are used, whether by being locked in open positions or lost in liquidated trades, the system <b>won’t open new trades</b> until more funds become available. This can lead to fewer trades being reflected in your stats compared to the number of signals posted on your channel. </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-image intercom-interblocks-align-center"><a href="https://downloads.intercomcdn.com/i/o/wrsw0nb0/1567821549/7dd94e6ea3b950e16aac16d26cbe/image.png?expires=1772919000&amp;signature=6df055f6b3999bad00112f20efd1a31781c65808fb3bf8620c74a51af80fb988&amp;req=dSUhEcF8nIRbUPMW1HO4za1WAL8c7QRLn9C4fSCxaOfBCfXai7jvhIcxuiuf%0A1Beyl5aptAO9Dr%2F6Yh8%3D%0A" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer nofollow noopener"><img src="https://downloads.intercomcdn.com/i/o/wrsw0nb0/1567821549/7dd94e6ea3b950e16aac16d26cbe/image.png?expires=1772919000&amp;signature=6df055f6b3999bad00112f20efd1a31781c65808fb3bf8620c74a51af80fb988&amp;req=dSUhEcF8nIRbUPMW1HO4za1WAL8c7QRLn9C4fSCxaOfBCfXai7jvhIcxuiuf%0A1Beyl5aptAO9Dr%2F6Yh8%3D%0A" width="414" height="243" style="width:370px"/></a></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-subheading intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h2 id="h_53a9dcf121">3. How often are the statistics and backtesting results updated?</h2></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>The backtesting results and channel statistics on the Marketplace are refreshed <b>once every 24 hours</b>.<br/>Please note that the update time may vary, there is <b>no fixed hour</b> for the refresh.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-subheading intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h2 id="h_30a00cff03">4. Why the PNL of my channel on the Marketplace is significantly different than my signal&#x27;s results on the channel? </h2></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>It’s important to understand that signal results are not the same as trade results. Signal results are <b>theoretical</b> and do not represent actual order execution on the exchange. On the other hand, trade results, like the ones shown in Marketplace backtesting, are calculated based on <b>real trading logic</b> that considers market conditions, exchange order book, and user configurations.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Here are some more reasons for differences between signal results and Marketplace Backtesting PNL:</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-unordered-nested-list"><ul><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p><b>Entry zone</b> - <br/>On signals, a target is considered fulfilled as soon as the price enters the entry zone. On trades (including backtesting), an entry strategy is applied, and not all orders may be filled within the zone.<br/>➡️ This means the <b>average entry price</b> is often different between signal results and trades.<br/>​</p></div></li><li><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p><b>Trailing configurations</b> - <br/>Trailing configurations are <b>applied only to trades</b>, not signals. Even if you add trailing to your signal, it only affects the trades created from it.<br/>➡️ As a result, trailing orders (Entry, Take-profits or Stop-loss movements) are <b>not included</b> in the signal’s theoretical PNL.</p></div></li></ul></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-subheading intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h2 id="h_1c7b31c114"><b>5. </b>Are the PNL results based on closed trades only, or both open and closed trades?<b> </b></h2></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>The PNL displayed on the Marketplace is calculated based on <b>both open and closed trades</b>, as they appear at the time of the closing the backtesting run (which is performed once every 24 hours).</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>This means that even trades that are still open can influence the current PNL, based on their unrealized profit or loss at the time the data was last updated.</p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p> </p></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-subheading intercom-interblocks-align-left"><h2 id="h_a899c2acd4">6. From which exchange are the backtesting results taken?</h2></div><div class="intercom-interblocks-paragraph no-margin intercom-interblocks-align-left"><p>Currently, the backtesting results are based on the <b>exchange that the channel posts the most signals for</b>.
